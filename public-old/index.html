About Tomasz Wostal

I’m a software architect with a passion for building innovative solutions in the medical industry. My expertise lies in DevOps practices, cloud infrastructure, and creating scalable, reliable systems that make a real impact on healthcare technology.

Throughout my career, I’ve developed a deep understanding of modern software architecture, combining traditional engineering principles with cutting-edge DevOps methodologies. My journey has taken me from hands-on development to architectural leadership, always with a focus on automation and efficiency.

What Drives Me

I’m passionate about the intersection of technology and healthcare. Building systems that can save lives and improve patient outcomes is incredibly rewarding. I believe in the power of automation, infrastructure as code, and continuous improvement to create robust, maintainable solutions.

My approach blends software architecture with DevOps culture. I don’t just design systems – I ensure they can be built, deployed, and maintained efficiently. This means embracing tools like Nix, containerization, CI/CD pipelines, and cloud-native technologies to create sustainable solutions.

When I’m not architecting systems, you might find me writing about DevOps on my blog at wostal.eu or pursuing my hobby as an aquarium enthusiast. I believe in sharing knowledge with the community and contributing to open-source projects. The same principles that make a balanced aquarium ecosystem work apply to well-designed distributed systems!
